Factions & People

The powers, tribes, and groups that shape life in Covaya.

Factions of Covaya

🏛️
The Covayan Compact
The government of Covaya — in theory and in practice, two different documents.

The Compact is Covaya's founding document and its live political situation. The High Chancellor in Copper City holds national authority. Regional governors hold local authority. 25 years years after the Shadow Wars, the question of who actually defers to whom is still being negotiated, region by region.

High Chancellor Nigel Trosbury
High Chancellor of Covaya

Appointed in E.o.C. 241 in a fractured election where half the governor council didn't participate. He knew from the moment of appointment that the harder work was not governing Covaya but reassembling it. He is stout in disposition and body. He does not abandon positions because they become difficult. He does not reframe failures as something else. In the immediate aftermath of the Shadow Wars, these qualities were exactly what the surviving governors were looking for. Whether they've been enough is a matter of perspective.

Current Tension

Some governors have never been brought back into meaningful compliance with the Compact. Trosbury has not yet found the lever that moves them — and his succession is an unresolved problem that political observers in Copper City discuss with increasing frequency as he ages.

The Quint — Wort & Tex
The original people of Covaya. Split during the Shadow Wars for reasons they haven't shared.

The Quint have been in Covaya longer than anyone else can document. Their own tradition does not specify a point of arrival — they do not describe themselves as people who came to the land. The distinction between arriving somewhere and being of it is one they hold seriously.

Their spiritual tradition requires no gods. It is less a religion than a relationship — with the land, its creatures, the seasons, the two moons. They observe. They tend. They feel the loss of the stars deeply. They learn the particular language of a place and speak it back. The knowledge lives in practice, not in text.

During the Shadow Wars, the Quint split. The Wort stayed in the northern jungle. The Tex moved south and east into the desert — into territory settlers and tribespeople had abandoned. Neither tribe has offered outsiders an explanation of why.

The Wort Tribe
Northern Jungle — Wort Woodlands

The Wort remained in the jungle they have occupied for longer than settler records reach. Their relationship with the Compact is one of mutual non-interference — the jungle is their boundary and the settlers learned early not to push through it. Their knowledge of the northern forest is unmatched and they will not share it.

The Tex Tribe
Great Tex Desert

The Tex moved into desert territory during the Shadow Wars — into land folks had abandoned and left to the strangeness of the south. They know the desert the way the Wort know the jungle. What drove them south is one of the more carefully kept secrets in Covaya, and outsiders who ask tend to get responses that navigate around the answer.

The Split

Outsiders know the split happened. Neither tribe has offered an explanation of why. It is known that Tex and Wort share a root tradition — but what each believes the other got wrong during the Shadow Wars is not yet known to anyone outside the tribes.

Law & Order (Frontier Edition)
On the frontier, the law is whoever is wearing the badge. Quality varies enormously.

Law on the frontier is a personal matter. There is no consistent standard, no independent judiciary, no extradition. The quality of justice in any given place is entirely dependent on the person wearing the badge — and on whether anyone has bothered to give them backup.

The Bandito Problem

Bandit activity has increased steadily across the frontier since the Shadow Wars. In towns too small to maintain a reliable militia and too remote for Compact intervention, bandits have learned to operate freely, sometimes even holding power. The pattern is consistent: test the limits of what local law can actually enforce, establish a presence, expand. Frontier towns know this pattern. Most of them are somewhere in the middle of it.
