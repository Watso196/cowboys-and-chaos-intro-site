A Settlement on the Snake River

Spine Gulch

Where the Mountains Meet the Desert — and Where the Campaign Begins

- Sheriff Aldous Crane of Spine Gulch

← Back to Map
Population:~100
Function:River trade stop
Government:Sheriff-Governor (one aging man)
Tone:Dusty, tired, honest, slightly haunted

Overview

Spine Gulch is the kind of town that exists because someone had to stop somewhere. It sits on the eastern bank of the Snake River just far enough north of the Strange Mountains to be habitable — which is a lower bar than it sounds. In the deep summer the Snake River shrinks to a brown trickle between cracked mud banks, and the town bakes.

The town barely survived the Shadow Wars. Several of its buildings did not, and nobody has torn down the empty ones or rebuilt them. They stand where they always stood, windows dark, doors hanging, slowly being reclaimed by the dust.

The town has no consistent armed force. The sheriff is getting old. The bandit problem along this stretch of desert is getting worse. Everyone in Spine Gulch is aware of both of these facts and most of them are managing a low-grade dread about what happens when the two facts meet properly.

✦

The Snake River

The Snake runs out of the Strange Mountains to the south, which explains both why Spine Gulch exists and why the river has always had a slightly unsettled reputation. The water runs colder than it should given the climate. Its color is an odd greenish-brown that locals attribute to mountain minerals and try not to think about too much.

The people of Spine Gulch drink from the Snake. They always have. There is no other reliable water source close enough to matter, and the river has never made anyone visibly sick. It may have done other, subtler things over the years. There are also rumors that something lives in the Snake River, but no one has proven them yet. There are rules for the Snake River here.

Key Locations

The Ramshackle

Spine Gulch's only bar — which means it is also its community hall, courthouse, rumor exchange, and primary form of entertainment. Dim even in daylight, smelling of woodsmoke and spilled liquor. The furniture is mismatched and heavily used. The bar itself is the nicest thing in the building, a long slab of dark wood that Billy the Bartender keeps cleaner than anything else in town. This is where the campaign will begin.

Billy Worst
Proprietor

Broad, weathered, sour. His disposition is simply his face. He dislikes everyone at roughly the same baseline level, which, once understood, is almost comforting. He remembers what people drink, cuts off drunks before they become problems, and keeps a bat behind the bar. He has used it regularly. If Billy spits in your drink, the town has collectively decided you are not welcome.

The Sheriff's Office

One room. One cell, currently empty. A desk, a gun rack with two rifles and a gap where a third used to be, a wanted board with just a couple notices. The office also handles tax collection, land claims, and anything else requiring an official stamp — all from the same battered desk.

Sheriff Aldous Crane
Sheriff-Governor

Twenty-two years as sheriff. He still knows the law, the land, and every person in town by name. What he is not, anymore, is fast. He is an old man who is tired and outpaced, which is in some ways harder to fix than corruption would be.

The Trading Post

The most functional building in Spine Gulch. A covered loading dock faces the river path; a front counter faces the main road. Handles the exchange of goods between river barges and town, buys and sells supplies for travelers, and serves as an informal bank — the owner keeps a locked strongbox behind the counter and might hold money for you, if you're trustworthy enough.

Dura Paths
Owner

Organized, plainspoken, with an accurate mental ledger of who owes what to whom in Spine Gulch. Not warm exactly — but fair, which in a town this size is more valuable. She and Billy Worst have a long-running disagreement about a debt that predates most current residents' memory and shows no sign of resolution.
