Copper City

Seat of the Covayan Compact. Largest city on the continent. The place where power lives — and where the money ultimately flows.

- Dr Wiley Snipes, Perfessor of Artifice-al Intelligence at the Copper City College of Arcane and Natural Sciences

← Back to Map
Population:~40,000
Economy:Copper mining & maritime trade
Government:High Chancellor Nigel Trosbury
Tone:Grand and somewhat grimy, ambitious and exhausted

Overview

Copper City is the largest settlement in Covaya by a considerable margin — forty thousand people in the city proper and its immediate surrounds. It is the seat of the Compact, home to the High Chancellor, and the place where the continent's money ultimately flows. Its harbor is the deepest on the coast. Its copper mines have been producing for generations and show no sign of exhaustion.

The Shadow Wars hit Copper City differently than the outlying territories. The city never fell — the harbor chains held, the walls held, the navy kept the worst incursions from the waterfront. What it lost was its hinterland. For nearly three decades Copper City was a wealthy fortress surrounded by a continent it could no longer govern. That experience left marks. The city's institutions are more intact than anywhere else in Covaya. They are also more inward-looking, more suspicious of the countryside, and considerably more accustomed to self-sufficiency than the Compact's founding documents anticipated.

At street level the city is a patchwork. Grand civic avenues give way to cramped neighborhoods three streets in any direction. The smell of the tanneries and smelting works drifts across the merchant quarter on a west wind. Forty thousand people means anonymity is possible — useful if you need to disappear, uncomfortable if you need help. The city runs on relationships, and a stranger with no history here exists in a particular social vacuum that is uncomfortable even when it is not dangerous.

The Districts

Copper City is too large and too old to be a single place. Six districts that grew into each other over two centuries, each with its own character and its own understanding of what the city is for.

🏛️
The Civic Quarter
Power & Politics

Stone streets, pale granite facades, copper-green patina on metalwork. The Chancellery dominates. The city's wealthiest old families live in quiet townhouses nearby. Scandals here are conducted quietly. Power does not need to announce itself.

Magistrate Sera Oult
Judge and Justice Server

The only magistrate whose reputation for impartiality is uncomplicated. Stalwart in her values and morals, she applies the law accurately and without favor — which is rarer than it sounds and worth more than it may appear.

⚓
The Harbor District
Trade & Informal Networks

Docks, cranes, warehouses, chandlers. Behind the waterfront: taverns, boarding houses, and establishments that don't advertise their nature. Operates on the tide and the manifest. Where strangers are least remarkable and arrangements can be made without witnesses.

Vera Cassant
Shipping Magnate

Sixty, lean, the largest single employer in the harbor district. Keeps meticulous records. Well known but never spoken of as a key player in Copper City's informal economy.

🔥
The Copper Works
Industry & Labor Tension

The smelting district sits upwind of most of the city. Furnaces run day and night — visible as a red glow on the ridge from the harbor. The mining families and the workers who run the operations have a relationship that is stable in the way things are stable just before they are not.

Aldric Hourn
Mining Patriarch

Large, loud, given to hospitality that functions as dominance. Does eliminate competitors rather than merely beat them, and maintains an intimate relationship with the magistrates who handle labor disputes. Considers the distinction between what he does and corruption to be meaningful.

⚖️
The Merchant Quarter
Commerce & Alithe's Shadow

Trading houses, banks, assayers, lawyers. The cracked hourglass of Alithe appears here more than anywhere else in the city — on lapels, signage, carved into doorframes. The merchants of Copper City are not people who examine the foundations of their beliefs too carefully.

🔬
The College District
Scholarship & Eccentricity

The Copper City College of Arcane and Natural Sciences occupies the eastern rise. From below it looks like respectable civic architecture. Up close it is something stranger: graduate students arguing planar theory in the street, experiments going occasionally and loudly wrong. The most cosmopolitan neighborhood in the city.

🕯️
The Temple District
Faith in Disarray

The great temple of Alithe has not been used for regular services in a generation. New faiths have moved into smaller shrine spaces. In one larger vacant building, something meets on certain nights that attendees describe in terms that don't quite add up.

🌉
The Channels
Residential — Where Most People Actually Live

Named for the compact feeling that all of these people living together brings. Dense, old, narrow streets and tall buildings. The cooking smells of a dozen culinary traditions on the same block. The Shadow Wars brought a significant population of displaced persons who never left. The Spans operates on its own social structure. The city guard patrols the main roads. The side streets are governed by mutual understanding.

Key Locations

The specific places that matter. The city has hundreds more.

🏛️
The Chancellery
Civic Quarter

Trosbury's seat. Public petitions on the ground floor, policy apparatus above, a basement that appears on no public document.

High Chancellor Nigel Trosbury
High Chancellor of Covaya

Twenty-three years in office. Plain-spoken, persistent, not naive. He knows the city has rot in it and has made strategic choices about what he addresses. His succession is the city's unspoken crisis.

📜
The Copper City Archive
Civic Quarter

The documentary record of Covayan history. A restricted collection requiring the head archivist's explicit permission.

Cornelia Areaux
Head Archivist

Meticulous, protective of documents that have survived things she considers it her professional responsibility to prevent them surviving again. Getting her trust is a longer project than bribing or intimidating her — neither of which will work.

🔬
The College of Arcane and Natural Sciences
College District

Old stone, newer annexes, outdoor workspaces in controlled disorder.

Professor Wiley Snipes
Chair, Artifice-al Intelligence Programme

He chairs the programme he founded, named himself, and runs on genuine brilliance and near-total organizational chaos. He will change the subject four times in a single sentence, forget the task he just assigned you, take a three-day detour that turns out to be entirely relevant in a way he cannot explain, and produce a working prototype of something that should not be possible on a timeline that makes no sense. The programme generates enough commercial research income that the college asks no further questions. You will find him maddening or endearing. Most people find him both.

🍺
The Mended Net
Harbor District

A tavern of middling quality and excellent location. The back room is theoretically for private dining.

Cutter
The Informal Economy

Nobody uses his real name. Thirty-five, slight, corner table. His prices are honest in the sense that he charges exactly what he says and delivers exactly what he promises — which makes him more reliable than most official institutions the players will encounter.

🌉
The Bridges Market
The Spans

Every fifth day on the middle Span. Goods of unclear provenance, information that cannot be obtained officially. Exists in the gap between what trade law covers and what it doesn't.

⏳
The Old Temple of Alithe
Temple District

Its copper dome has gone fully green. Doors unlocked, interior dim and cold. The back rooms contain records nobody has fully catalogued in thirty years.

Arriving in Copper City

The first thing travelers notice, arriving by sea, is the smell of copper — metallic and slightly sweet, stronger on warm days. Then the harbor: busy in a way that Spine Gulch is not and cannot be. The civic quarter, reached by the main road from the harbor gates, is the city presenting its best face. Stone. Lamp posts. People who dress better than anywhere else in Covaya and watch strangers more carefully.

The city runs on relationships — who vouches for you, who you owe, who owes you. A stranger with no voucher and no history exists in a social vacuum that is uncomfortable even when it is not dangerous. Finding a way out of that vacuum is the first practical challenge Copper City presents.

- Dr Wiley Snipes, Perfessor of Artifice-al Intelligence at the Copper City College of Arcane and Natural Sciences
