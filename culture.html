Culture & Life in Covaya

What it means to live on the frontier.

The Covayan Compact

- High Chancellor Nigel Trosbury

What the government says it is, and what it actually is, are two related but distinct things.

How It's Supposed to Work

At the top sits the High Chancellor in Copper City, with authority over matters that cross regional lines — external relations, naval command, inter-regional trade, currency. Below sit regional governors, each handling local law, taxation, and militia in their own territory. The High Chancellor speaks for Covaya as a nation. The governors are largely left alone.

When the system works, it's elegant — flexible enough for a continent with wildly different regions, resilient enough that one region's failure doesn't collapse the whole.

How It Actually Works

The system functions on the assumption that Copper City holds enough economic and military leverage to make deference worthwhile. When that leverage is strong, the Compact works reasonably well. When it weakens, as it has, governors operate as independent rulers and the High Chancellor governs whatever Copper City can physically reach.

After nearly three decades of the Shadow Wars, leverage is low. Some governors have returned to cooperation. Some are negotiating. Some haven't responded at all.

The Honest Summary

The Compact works well enough when things are going well and poorly when they are not. Right now, the Compact feels distant and weak, and everyone knows it.

Justice Is Variable

There is no common legal standard across Covaya. What a person can expect from the law depends almost entirely on where they happen to be standing. What's legal in one town could be a hanging offense in the next.

No Extradition

There is no functioning extradition agreement between regions. A fugitive who crosses into a different governorship is practically safe unless the two governors have a cooperative relationship — and in the current climate, many do not.

Law on the Ground

For most Covayans, the law is whoever is wearing the badge in the town they're standing in.

Sheriffs & Marshals

Local law enforcement is appointed at the governor's pleasure or emerged from necessity. Quality varies wildly. In frontier towns, the sheriff is often one tired man holding things together through reputation alone. In towns with more resources, officers might be appointed.

Militias

Governors are expected to maintain a regional militia. Some do. Some let them lapse or lost them during the Shadow Wars and never rebuilt them. The Compact provides no mechanism to compel this aside from angering the Chancellery.

The Chancellery Court

The only consistent court in Covaya handles crimes that cross regional lines — smuggling, inter-regional fraud, treason. It is the most reliable and the most subject to political pressure.

Capital Punishment

Requires a governor's explicit authorization in most regional codes. What constitutes a capital crime varies considerably from region to region. Whether an officer or militia will wait for authorization is up for debate.

The Gods, Such As They Are

- Father Benny Bunce of the Metaphysical Church of Copper City

The gods of Covaya are not what they were. The God War, the breaking of the Transcosmic Divide, and the Shadow Wars have left the divine order fractured and religion in disarray. Knowing a god's name and having that god answer are two different things, and the second happens rarely.

✦
⛈️
Eia, the Stormcaller
Old God | Sky, Air, Storms

Still worshipped by sailors and farmers out of necessity rather than piety. Her worship is practical — the way you'd respect a dangerous neighbor. Whether she hears prayers anymore is a matter of genuine theological debate among her own priests.

🌙
Mahina, the Nightwarden
Old God | Moons, Cycles

Since the stars went out, the moons are the only reliable lights in the night sky. Mahina's following has grown accordingly. Her altars are the most consistently tended in Covaya. Whether she is active is arguable, but when travellers make it home safely they pay her tribute.

☀️
Asteri, the Sundowner
Old God | Sun, Dust

One of the few old gods whose following has genuinely grown since the God War. The sun still rises. That's more than most gods can promise. Her Dust domain carries a quiet theology: everything ends, everything is ground down, and there is no shame in this. Frontier folks appreciate consistency.

⚖️
Lady Luck
Luck, Balance

Though Lady Luck is not widely worshipped, she is known. Gamblers leave coins on windowsills. Midwives invoke her name. People who've survived something they probably shouldn't have sometimes build small shrines without quite knowing who they are thanking.

📖
Alpinwell, The Wayfarer
Journey, Trial

Nobody knows exactly how he became a god. He walked across Covaya for a cause, gathered people along the way, and something happened. Every one of his followers has a story that begins with him walking through a door uninvited, which leads them to a purpose.

⏳
Alithe, the Unmasked
Old God | Ambition, Legacy

Once the most widely worshipped god in the known world — the god of Truth, Honor, and Trust. It was a lie. When his long manipulation came to light, many turned on him, potentially other gods. He still has followers in Copper City, particularly among the nobility and merchant class. They may not fully understand how precarious their god's position actually is.

✦

The Way of the World

The two native tribes of Covaya do not worship gods. This is not a gap in their spiritual life — it is a considered position. Their tradition is less a religion than a relationship: with the land, with its creatures, with the cycles of weather and season. Practitioners do not pray. They observe. They tend. They learn the particular language of the place they inhabit and speak it back accurately. It requires enormous skill and discipline to maintain.

Daily Life on the Frontier

The Frontier Character

Covayans are stubborn, practical, and hospitable to strangers in the way of people who understand that life is hard. They don't complain much because there isn't anyone to complain to. They extend help because that's what you do.

Distrust of Authority

Most frontier folk don't trust the Compact or the Chancellery. They called for help during the Shadow Wars and received nothing back. Don't come calling if you aren't willing to answer.

The Shadow Wars' Legacy

People know whose parents and grandparents died to shadow creatures and what years those deaths happened in. They talk about it the way people talk about weather — not constantly, but readily, because it is the shared fact of their lives. A sense of trauma bonding.

The Starless Sky

Not many people alive have seen the stars. Religion, navigation, agriculture — all of it reorganized around the two moons fifty years ago. The moons are not a replacement. They are simply what's left. Sometimes they don't feel like enough.
